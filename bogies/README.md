# BOGIES

**UNIDENTIFIED CONTACTS. HOSTILE INTENT. PERFECT TIMING REQUIRED.**

In the Starfleet Padawan Academy at SpaceX, "bogies" are the simulated (or sometimes very real) threats that appear on scopes during the most critical moments of training.

## Context in the Saga

The academy's curriculum has evolved through several layers of existential training:

- **Punch the Glass**: When protocols fail, shatter the barrier.
- **Monke Button**: Make monke not kill monke. Then feed the monke. Then let the monke rest.
- **Neverender Protocol** (Tame Impala x Justice): COMMENCE the infinite psych-electro loop. The road never ends.
- **Right on Cue**: The master timing device that ensures everything happens at the exact perfect moment.

Now the universe has introduced **BOGIES**.

These are the contacts that pop up "right on cue" during Neverender loops, during Forge sessions, during Monke Button activations. They test whether the cadets (and the monkes) have truly internalized the lessons.

## What Are Bogies?

- Unidentified radar returns that could be:
  - Hostile drones
  - Rogue monke factions
  - Glitches in the Neverender simulation
  - Or something worse (the academy doesn't ask too many questions)
- They appear when the system is at its most vulnerable: mid-peace resolution, deep in the loop, or exactly when the Right on Cue sweeper is in the green zone.
- Engaging them requires the full toolkit:
  - Monke-level calm (no killing, just precise action)
  - Academy precision (Punch the Glass timing on weapons lock)
  - Neverender rhythm (hit the targets in sync with the beat)
  - Right on Cue execution (fire only when the light is green)

## The Bogies Protocol

1. **Scan** — Radar lights up. Multiple bogies. "BOGIES, BOGIES, BOGIES."
2. **Calm the Monke** — Use the Monke Button if things get heated. Feed and rest as needed.
3. **Neverender Sync** — Drop into the loop. Let the Tame Impala waves and Justice pulses guide the targeting.
4. **Right on Cue** — Wait for the perfect window. Lock. Engage. No early shots. No late shots.
5. **After-Action** — Debrief with the Council. The monkes that were tired and hungry are now full, rested, and victorious.

## Integration

Bogies are the stress test for the entire system. A cadet who has mastered Punch the Glass but panics when bogies appear fails. A monke who achieved peace but reverts to angry mode under bogie pressure needs more button time. A Neverender loop that desyncs when bogies show up needs deeper Right on Cue calibration.

The protocol is simple in theory:
**Stay monke. Stay on beat. Hit the cue.**
